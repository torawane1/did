# Study in Denmark

Denmark is home to world-class educational institutions known for their student-centered learning, emphasis on critical thinking, and collaboration. For Indian students, it offers a high standard of living, English-medium degree programs, and attractive post-graduation work opportunities.

---

## Choosing Your Degree

### 1. Bachelor’s Degrees
*   **Duration:** 3 years (180 ECTS).
*   **Language of Instruction:** The vast majority of Bachelor's programs are taught in **Danish**. Only a very small selection of programs (primarily in engineering, business, and IT) are offered in English.
*   **Requirements:** High School Certificate (12th standard) equivalent to the Danish secondary school education, along with specific course prerequisites (e.g., mathematics at a certain level).

### 2. Master’s Degrees (Kandidat)
*   **Duration:** 2 years (120 ECTS).
*   **Language of Instruction:** There is a wide variety of English-taught Master's programs across all major universities.
*   **Requirements:** A completed, relevant Bachelor’s degree from a recognized university. Course-to-course mapping is strict — your Bachelor's syllabus must match the ECTS credit requirements of the Danish Master's program prerequisites.

## Admission Process & Deadlines

### Key Deadlines
*   **Autumn Intake (September Start):**
    *   **Non-EU/EEA applicants:** The deadline is typically **January 15** or **March 1** (varies by university). Always apply early to leave enough time for university evaluation and visa processing.
*   **Spring Intake (February Start):**
    *   Not all programs offer a February intake. The deadline for non-EU students is usually **September 1** of the preceding year.

### Application Portals
*   **For Bachelor's Degrees:** Apply through the national portal [www.optagelse.dk](https://www.optagelse.dk).
*   **For Master's Degrees:** Apply directly through the university's online application system (DANS portal or the university's own application system).

### Core Documents Needed
*   [ ] **Academic Transcripts & Degree Certificates:** Translated into English.
*   [ ] **Course Syllabus:** Detailed description of courses taken during your Bachelor's to prove credit equivalency.
*   [ ] **English Language Proficiency:**
    *   IELTS Academic: Minimum score of **6.5** overall (some courses require higher).
    *   TOEFL iBT: Minimum score of **83–90**.
*   [ ] **Curriculum Vitae (CV) & Statement of Purpose (SoP)**.
*   [ ] **Letters of Recommendation** (optional but highly recommended for scholarships).

## Tuition Fees & Scholarships

### Tuition Fees
Higher education in Denmark is free for EU/EEA students. Non-EU/EEA students must pay tuition fees:
*   **Average Cost:** **€6,000 to €16,000 per year** (approx. **DKK 45,000 to DKK 120,000**). Technical and medical degrees are generally more expensive than business or humanities degrees.
*   **Application Fee:** Most Danish universities charge an application fee of around **DKK 750 (approx. €100)** for non-EU applicants to process applications.

### Scholarships & Funding
*   **Danish Government Scholarships:** Awarded to highly qualified non-EU students. They come in the form of a **full or partial tuition fee waiver**, and sometimes include a **monthly stipend** to cover living expenses. There is no separate application; you are automatically considered when you apply for admission.
*   **Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees:** Fully funded scholarships for specific joint master's programs across multiple European universities.
*   **University-Specific Aid:** Some institutions offer targeted tuition waivers for top-ranking international applicants.

## Student Residence Permit (Visa)

Once you receive your university acceptance letter (and pay your first semester's tuition fee), the university will initiate your residence permit application with SIRI (Danish Agency for International Recruitment and Integration).

### Visa Checklist
*   [ ] **ST1 Application Form:** Initiated by the university, completed by you.
*   [ ] **Case Order ID & Fee Payment:** Paid to SIRI (approx. **DKK 2,100 - 2,500**).
*   [ ] **Proof of Financial Self-Sufficiency:** As a non-EU student, you must prove you have enough funds to support yourself in Denmark. Currently, you need to show you possess approx. **DKK 6,800 per month** of study (usually around **DKK 81,600** for a full academic year).
*   [ ] **Biometrics:** You must visit a Danish embassy, consulate, or visa application center (VFS) to record your biometrics.

### Working While Studying
*   You are legally permitted to work up to **20 hours per week** during your semester studies.
*   You can work **full-time (37 hours per week)** during the summer holidays in June, July, and August.
*   **Note:** Finding a part-time job in Denmark without speaking Danish can be challenging. Common options include food delivery, warehousing, student assistant roles in multinational tech companies, and service industries.

## Student Accommodation & Dormitories (Kollegier)

Danish universities generally **do not provide on-campus housing**. Finding accommodation is entirely your responsibility. Start searching the moment you submit your university application.

### Student Housing Portals (Kollegier)
Student residence halls (Kollegier) are the most affordable and social housing option. Rent typically ranges from **DKK 2,500 to DKK 5,000 per month** (including utilities).

*   **Copenhagen & Greater Area:**
    *   [KKIK (Kollegiernes Kontor i København)](https://kollegierneskontor.dk) — Free queue system for thousands of rooms.
    *   [CIU (Centralindstillingsudvalget)](https://ciu.dk) — Central application system for student apartments and rooms.
    *   [S.dk](https://s.dk) — Joint platform for student and youth housing.
*   **Aarhus:**
    *   [StudentHousingAarhus.com](https://studenthousingaarhus.com) — The official student housing portal for Aarhus.
*   **Odense:**
    *   [Studiebolig Odense](https://studiebolig-odense.dk) — Central portal for student housing in Odense.
*   **Aalborg:**
    *   [Aalborg Studiebolig](https://aalborgstudiebolig.dk) — Portal for student housing in Aalborg.

### Private Rentals & Short-term Housing
*   **Private Portals:** [BoligPortal.dk](https://boligportal.dk) or [Lejebolig.dk](https://lejebolig.dk). These require a subscription fee to message landlords.
*   **Sublets & Rooms:** Websites like **HousingAnywhere** or Facebook student groups (search for "Roommate in Copenhagen", etc.).
*   > ⚠️ **Avoid Scams:** Never transfer money before signing a contract (Lejekontrakt) or before seeing the room in person (or having a trusted contact inspect it). Always use secure payment options where possible.

## Post-Graduation: The 3-Year Job-Seeking Permit

In 2023, the Danish government simplified rules for international graduates to retain talent.

*   **Automatic 3-Year Job-Seeking Permit:** Non-EU graduates who complete an accredited Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD program in Denmark are automatically granted a **3-year residence permit for job seeking** (replacing the old "Establishment Card" scheme).
*   **Unlimited Work Rights:** During these 3 years, you have full work rights. You do not need to apply for a separate work permit. You can work in any field, change jobs freely, or start your own business.
*   **Transition to Work Visa:** Once you find a job matching your qualifications, you can transition to the Pay Limit Scheme or Positive List work permit to accumulate years toward permanent residency.
