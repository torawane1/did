# Tourism & Travel

Denmark is a beautiful Scandinavian country that seamlessly blends historic castles, stunning natural landscapes, and cutting-edge modern design. Whether you are a resident looking to explore or a visiting student on a budget, this guide covers everything you need to navigate Danish tourism.

---

## Must-Visit Attractions: Copenhagen

Copenhagen is a compact, bike-friendly capital with plenty of historical and modern attractions. 

### 1. Iconic Sights
*   **Nyhavn:** The iconic 17th-century canal lined with colorful townhouses, historic wooden ships, and cafes. Great for a walk or a classic canal boat tour (DFDS or Netto-Bådene).
*   **Tivoli Gardens:** One of the world’s oldest operating amusement parks. Famous for its vintage roller coasters, lush gardens, and beautiful light displays at night (especially during Halloween and Christmas).
*   **The Round Tower (Rundetårn):** Built in the 17th century as an observatory. You walk up a unique spiral ramp for panoramic views of Copenhagen.
*   **Amalienborg & Rosenborg Castles:** Amalienborg is the winter residence of the Royal Family (watch the Changing of the Guard at noon). Rosenborg houses the dazzling Danish Crown Jewels.
*   **Freetown Christiania:** A self-governing, alternative community in the Christianshavn district known for its unique houses, art galleries, organic cafes, and green lakes.

### 2. Free or Budget Experiences in the Capital
*   **The King’s Garden (Kongens Have):** A beautiful park surrounding Rosenborg Castle, perfect for picnics.
*   **The Botanical Garden (Botanisk Have):** Located in the center, featuring free public gardens. The historic glass Palm House has a small entry fee.
*   **Islands Brygge Harbour Bath:** Clean public swimming pools directly in the city harbor. Safe, monitored, and completely free to swim in during the summer months.
*   **Dragør Village:** A historic 18th-century yellow-walled fishing village located just 30 minutes south of Copenhagen by bus. Ideal for a quiet coastal escape.

## Must-Visit Attractions: Outside Copenhagen

Denmark's efficient train network makes it easy to take memorable day trips.

### 1. Royal Castles & Viking History (Zealand)
*   **Frederiksborg Castle (Hillerød):** Located 40 minutes north of Copenhagen. A spectacular Renaissance palace situated on three islets in the Castle Lake, surrounded by a baroque garden.
*   **Kronborg Castle (Helsingør):** Known worldwide as the setting of Shakespeare's *Hamlet*. Located at the narrowest point of the sound between Denmark and Sweden.
*   **Roskilde Cathedral & Viking Ship Museum:** Located 25 minutes west of Copenhagen. Roskilde Cathedral is a UNESCO site housing the tombs of Danish monarchs. The nearby museum features five original 11th-century Viking ships.

### 2. Nature & Landscapes
*   **Dyrehaven (The Deer Park):** Located in Klampenborg (20 mins north of CPH). A UNESCO park where thousands of red and fallow deer roam freely among ancient oak trees.
*   **Møns Klint:** Dramatic white chalk cliffs rising 120 meters out of the Baltic Sea, surrounded by beech forests. Located on the island of Møn (best accessed by car or organized day tours).

### 3. Beyond Zealand (Aarhus, Odense & Jutland)
*   **Odense (Fyn):** The birthplace of Hans Christian Andersen. Features fairy-tale cobbled streets and the state-of-the-art **H.C. Andersens Hus** museum. (1.5 hours by train from CPH).
*   **Aarhus (Jutland):** Denmark's second-largest city. Must-sees include the **ARoS Aarhus Art Museum** (famous for the rainbow panorama walkway) and **Den Gamle By** (The Old Town), a living history museum.
*   **Skagen:** The northernmost tip of Jutland where the Kattegat (Baltic) and Skagerrak (North Sea) oceans collide. You can stand with one foot in each sea at Grenen sandbar.
*   **Billund:** The home of LEGO. Ideal for visiting Legoland and the Lego House.

## Student Discounts & Saving Money

Denmark is expensive, but students can save significant amounts by using these hacks:

### 1. Sightseeing & Culture
*   **Museum Discounts:** Almost all major museums (such as the National Museum of Denmark, Glyptoteket, and the SMK Art Museum) offer **10% to 50% discounts** for students. Always carry your university student card or an International Student Identity Card (ISIC).
*   **The K7 Program:** If you are under 27, you can sign up for the **K7 pass**, which grants free or extremely cheap entry to over 180 museums across Denmark during specific campaign weeks (typically in mid-September).
*   **Free Museum Days:** Some museums offer free entry on specific days (e.g., Glyptoteket is free on the last Wednesday of every month).

### 2. Apps & Food Deals
*   **Studiz App:** The national portal for student discounts. Download the app to find student deals on restaurants, tech, clothing, and local coffee shops.
*   **Too Good To Go:** An app where bakeries, supermarkets, and restaurants sell surplus food at 70% off at the end of the day. A major favorite for students in Denmark.

### 3. Public Transport Discounts
*   **Copenhagen Card:** If you have friends or family visiting for 24-120 hours, get the Copenhagen Card. It provides unlimited travel on buses, metro, and trains in the entire capital region (Zones 1-99, including CPH Airport and trips to Hillerød/Helsingør) plus free entry to 80+ sights.
*   **DSB Ungdomskort / DSB Youth Tickets:** If you are under 26 or a student, you qualify for youth train tickets, which give up to **33% discount** on intercity train travel across the country.

## What to Prepare

*   [ ] **100% Cashless Travel:** Denmark is a card-only society. You do not need Danish Krone cash. Ensure you have a Visa or Mastercard debit/credit card with zero or low foreign transaction fees.
*   [ ] **Bring Layers:** Danish weather is notoriously unpredictable. A sunny day can turn into a cold, rainy, and windy afternoon within minutes. Always carry a windproof and waterproof jacket, regardless of the season.
*   [ ] **Install Essential Apps:**
    *   **Rejseplanen:** The ultimate app for planning public transport journeys.
    *   **DOT Billetter:** For buying single tickets, commuter passes, or city passes.
    *   **DontPayTax (for tax-free shopping):** If you are visiting on a tourist visa from India, you can get a VAT refund on purchases over DKK 300.
*   [ ] **Biking Prep:** If you rent a bike (via Donkey Republic or Swapfiets), study the local cycling hand signals:
    *   Raise your hand to signal you are stopping.
    *   Point left or right before turning.
    *   **Copenhagen Left:** To turn left at an intersection, cross straight to the opposite corner, stop, turn your bike 90 degrees, and wait for the light to turn green in your new direction.

## What to Be Aware Of (Warnings & Pitfalls)

*   **⚠️ Stay Out of Bike Lanes:** Walking or standing in a bike lane is highly dangerous and considered rude. Cyclists ride fast and expect pedestrians to stay on the sidewalk. Always look both ways before crossing a bike path.
*   **⚠️ Pedestrian Red Lights:** Danes strictly obey traffic lights. Jaywalking (crossing the street on a red pedestrian light) is illegal and can result in an immediate **DKK 700 fine**.
*   **⚠️ Freetown Christiania Safety:** Freetown Christiania has local rules, particularly around Pusher Street. Do not take photos or videos in this specific area (follow the signs), do not run, and do not buy or carry illegal substances outside the gates, as police regularly conduct checks.
*   **⚠️ Pickpocketing Hotspots:** While Denmark is generally safe, professional pickpockets operate in tourist crowds, especially around Copenhagen Central Station, Nørreport Station, Nyhavn, Strøget, and airport baggage claim areas. Keep your backpack zipped and watch your phone.
*   **⚠️ Sunday Closures:** Danish shops close early. Most supermarkets close by 8 PM or 10 PM, and retail shops on Strøget often close by 3 PM or 4 PM on Sundays. Check opening hours ahead of time.
