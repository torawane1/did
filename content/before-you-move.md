# Before You Move

Everything you need to know before you book your flight — from choosing the right visa type to negotiating your offer and planning your timeline.

---

## Visa Types

Denmark has two primary work permit routes for Indians moving for employment. Your employer will usually advise which one applies, but it helps to understand both. If you are moving for studies, see the [Study in Denmark](#student-guide) section for student visa details.

### 1. The Positive List (Positivlisten)

The Positive List is a government-maintained list of job titles where there is a shortage of qualified workers in Denmark. If your job title is on the list, you qualify for a fast-track work permit.

**Key facts:**
- Updated by the Danish Agency for Labour Market and Recruitment (STAR) every 6 months
- No minimum salary requirement (but your offer must be market-rate)
- Includes most software engineering, data, and infrastructure roles
- Processing time: typically **1–3 months** via SIRI (the Danish Immigration Service)
- Your employer must be **certified** or apply on your behalf

**Commonly listed tech titles (as of 2026):**
- Software Developer / Software Engineer
- IT Architect
- Data Scientist / Machine Learning Engineer
- DevOps / Cloud Engineer
- Cybersecurity Specialist
- IT Project Manager

> **Check the current list**: [www.workindenmark.dk/positivlisten](https://www.workindenmark.dk) — titles rotate in/out, so always verify before assuming you qualify.

---

### 2. The Pay Limit Scheme (Beløbsgrænseordningen)

If your job title is not on the Positive List, the Pay Limit Scheme is the alternative. You qualify if your annual salary exceeds a minimum threshold set by the government.

**Key facts (2026 figures — verify at SIRI):**
- Standard Pay Limit: **DKK 448,000/year** (gross, ~DKK 37,333/month)
- Fast-track Pay Limit: **DKK 629,000/year** (gross) — priority processing
- Applies to any job title
- No employer certification required (but the company must be registered in Denmark)
- Processing: standard track ~1–3 months, fast-track ~1 month

**Who typically uses this route:**
- Senior/Staff engineers with high compensation
- Roles not covered by the Positive List
- Professionals at startups or SMEs that may not be SIRI-certified

---

### 3. EU Blue Card

Less common for Indian nationals but worth knowing — the EU Blue Card is an EU-wide work permit for highly qualified non-EU workers.

- Requires a **recognised university degree** (Bachelor's minimum in a relevant field)
- Salary threshold: typically the same or similar to the Pay Limit Scheme
- Advantage: easier intra-EU mobility if you later move to another EU country

Most Indian tech workers use Positivlisten or Pay Limit rather than Blue Card — your employer/immigration lawyer will advise.

## Required Documents

Regardless of which route your employer uses, you'll need to gather the following. Start collecting these **as soon as you receive your offer letter**.

### From You (the applicant)

- [ ] Valid **passport** (ideally 2+ years remaining validity)
- [ ] **Educational certificates** — degree(s) and transcripts, often requiring apostille/attestation
- [ ] **Work experience letters** — from all previous employers, on letterhead with dates and role
- [ ] **Proof of employment** — signed offer letter from your Danish employer
- [ ] **Recent payslips** — from current employer (last 3 months)
- [ ] **Passport-size photos** — usually 2–4 required
- [ ] Filled **SIRI application form** (your employer or lawyer will prepare this)

### From Your Employer (they handle this)

- [ ] Signed **employment contract** (Danish and/or English)
- [ ] **Company registration documents** (CVR number proof)
- [ ] **Employer declaration** confirming compliance with Danish employment conditions
- [ ] Certification documents (if applying via Positive List certified route)

### Document Attestation Notes

- Indian **degree certificates** typically need to be attested by the **Ministry of External Affairs (MEA)** in India and then by the **Danish Embassy in India** — plan 2–4 weeks for this
- Work experience letters should ideally be **notarised** — requirements vary, check with your SIRI application
- **Apostille** is not part of the Hague Convention for Denmark ↔ India documents in all cases — check current requirements at the Danish Embassy website

## Offer Negotiation

Danish compensation norms differ significantly from US/UK/India tech markets. Here's what to know before negotiating.

### Salary Benchmarks (Copenhagen, 2026 estimates)

| Level | Annual Gross (DKK) | Approx. EUR/month |
|---|---|---|
| Mid-level (3–5 yrs) | 550,000 – 700,000 | 6,100 – 7,800 |
| Senior (5–10 yrs) | 700,000 – 900,000 | 7,800 – 10,000 |
| Staff / Principal | 900,000 – 1,200,000 | 10,000 – 13,300 |
| Engineering Manager | 800,000 – 1,100,000 | 8,900 – 12,200 |

> These are estimates. Salaries vary significantly by company size, sector, and role. Startups often pay lower cash but offer equity. Large corporations (Maersk, Novo Nordisk) offer higher base with strong benefits.

### What's Included in a Danish Package

- **Base salary** — negotiable
- **Pension contribution** — employer typically contributes 8–17% on top of salary (this is *not* included in the gross salary you see — it's additional). Negotiate for clarity.
- **Holiday pay** — 5 weeks mandatory paid holiday by law (some companies offer 6)
- **Health insurance** — most companies offer private supplemental health insurance (top-up to public system)
- **Phone/laptop** — standard
- **Relocation allowance** — common for international hires, usually DKK 10,000–30,000 or one month's rent
- **Stock / RSUs** — less common than US tech, but growing at startups and scale-ups

### Negotiation Tips

- **Pension is additive**: When comparing offers, add the employer pension contribution to the gross salary for a true comparison. A DKK 600K salary with 15% pension is actually DKK 690K total cost to employer.
- **Negotiating is expected** — Danes are direct. It's not rude to counter an offer. Simply say: *"I was hoping for X — is there flexibility?"*
- **Tax matters a lot**: At senior salaries, you'll pay ~55–56% effective tax. Your **take-home** is significantly lower than gross. Use [Skat's income calculator](https://skat.dk) to estimate net pay before comparing to other countries.
- **Ask about the notice period** in the contract — most Danish employment contracts have 1–6 month notice periods on both sides (see [Work Culture](#work-culture)).

## Relocation Timeline

Here's a realistic timeline from offer acceptance to being settled in Denmark.

| Timeframe | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Day 1 (Offer accepted)** | Request employer starts SIRI application; begin gathering documents |
| **Week 1–2** | MEA attestation of degree in India; draft and sign employment contract |
| **Week 2–4** | SIRI application submitted by employer |
| **Week 4–12** | SIRI processing period (varies — track at [siri.dk](https://www.nyidanmark.dk)) |
| **On approval** | Book flights; arrange temporary accommodation in Denmark |
| **First week in Denmark** | Register address at Borgerservice; get CPR number (see [First 30 Days](#first-30-days)) |
| **Week 2–4 in Denmark** | Open bank account, activate MitID, get SIM card, start apartment hunting |

> **Buffer generously**: SIRI timelines are notoriously variable. Do not resign from your current job or book non-refundable travel until you have the **approved residence permit** in hand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I apply for a visa before I have a job offer?**
No — Danish work permits are employer-sponsored. You need a signed offer to apply.

**Can I do a recce trip while waiting for the permit?**
Yes — Indian passport holders can visit Denmark visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period (Schengen rules). Use this to apartment-hunt or meet your future team.

**Does my spouse automatically get the right to work?**
Yes — once your work permit is approved, your spouse can apply for a family reunification permit that **includes the right to work** in Denmark. See [Long Term](#long-term).

**What if my job title isn't on the Positive List?**
Use the Pay Limit Scheme if your salary exceeds the threshold (DKK 448,000/year as of 2026). Confirm the current threshold at [SIRI](https://www.nyidanmark.dk).
