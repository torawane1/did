## How to Use This Site

Each section is written as a **scannable reference** — not a narrative blog post. Use the sidebar to jump between topics. Use the search bar to find specific terms (CPR, MitID, positivlisten, etc.). Every page shows a "Last Updated" date so you can judge freshness.

> **Always verify at official sources.** Denmark's immigration rules, tax rates, and procedures change. Cross-reference anything critical at [SIRI](https://www.nyidanmark.dk), [SKAT](https://www.skat.dk), and [WorkInDenmark](https://www.workindenmark.dk).

## Quick Reference: The Denmark Tech Job Landscape

- 🇩🇰 **Capital**: Copenhagen (Scandinavia's largest tech hub)
- 💶 **Currency**: Danish Krone (DKK). 1 EUR ≈ 7.46 DKK (2026)
- 🗣️ **Language**: Danish — but tech workplaces are overwhelmingly English
- 💻 **Tech scene**: Strong in fintech (Lunar, Pleo, Nets), shipping/logistics (Maersk, Flexport DK), pharma-tech (Novo Nordisk Digital), and gaming (IO Interactive)
- ⏰ **Timezone**: CET (UTC+1 in winter, UTC+2 in summer)
- 🌡️ **Climate**: Cold winters (0–5°C), mild summers (20–25°C). Prepare for grey skies Oct–March
- 🏥 **Healthcare**: Universal public healthcare — free at the point of use after CPR registration
- 🎓 **International community**: ~15,000 Indian nationals live in Denmark, concentrated in Copenhagen, Aarhus, and Odense

## Common First Questions

**Do I need to speak Danish to work?**
No. Most tech companies operate in English. However, Danish helps enormously for daily life, housing applications, and long-term integration. Consider starting classes once settled — they're subsidised.

**Is Denmark expensive?**
Yes, but salaries are calibrated to the cost of living. Expect Copenhagen rents of DKK 8,000–15,000/month for a 1-bedroom. Groceries and dining are expensive by Indian standards. Most people find the quality of life worth the cost.

**How long does visa processing take?**
Typically 1–3 months for a work permit under the Positivlisten or Pay Limit Scheme, though SIRI timelines vary. See [Before You Move](#before-you-move) for details.

**Can I bring my family?**
Yes. Spouses and dependent children can apply for family reunification. See [Long Term](#long-term) for details on family dependent visas.
