## How to Use This Site

Each section is written as a **scannable reference** — not a narrative blog post. Use the sidebar to jump between topics. Press **⌘K** (or Ctrl+K) to search for any topic. Every section page shows a "Last Updated" date so you can judge freshness.

If you are coming to Denmark for higher studies (Bachelor's or Master's), check out our dedicated [Study in Denmark](#student-guide) section for admissions, tuition, and housing resources.

> **Always verify at official sources.** Denmark's immigration rules, study permits, tax rates, and procedures change. Cross-reference anything critical at [SIRI](https://www.nyidanmark.dk), [SKAT](https://www.skat.dk), and [WorkInDenmark / Study in Denmark](https://www.workindenmark.dk).

## Common First Questions

**Do I need to speak Danish to work or study?**
No. Most tech companies and university Master's programs operate in English. However, Danish helps enormously for daily life, housing applications, and long-term integration. Consider starting subsidised language classes once settled.

**Is Denmark expensive?**
Yes, but salaries are calibrated to cost of living. Expect Copenhagen rents of DKK 8,000–15,000/month for a private 1-bedroom, or DKK 3,000–5,000/month for student housing (Kollegier). Most people find the quality of life worth the cost.

**How long does visa processing take?**
Typically 1–3 months for a work permit under the Positivlisten or Pay Limit Scheme, and 2–3 months for a student residence permit. See [Before You Move](#before-you-move) or [Study in Denmark](#student-guide) for details.

**Can I stay in Denmark after graduation?**
Yes. Graduates of accredited Danish higher education institutions are automatically granted a **3-year job-seeking permit** (post-study work permit) to look for a job or start a business. See [Study in Denmark](#student-guide) for more information.

**Can I bring my family?**
Yes. Spouses and dependent children can apply for family reunification. This applies to both work permit holders and master's/PhD students (conditions apply). See [Long Term](#long-term) for family dependent visa details.

**Can my spouse work?**
Yes — once family reunification is approved, your spouse gets full right to work in Denmark with no separate work permit needed.
